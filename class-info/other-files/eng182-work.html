Hi there! My name is Sophia Hernandez and I am currently a freshman at the University of Washington (UW). I am studying under the majors of Education, Communities, and Organizations (ECO) and Political Science. As one can imagine, my studies and career will be filled with lots of reading and writing, so understanding how to analyze text and create forms of communication will be extremely useful. 

Throughout my high school years, I always dreaded having to read such dense novels and analyzing text that I did not understand. When I registered for my first set of classes at the UW, I cannot say that I was thrilled to be enrolled in an English course at the start of my college career, especially an Honors level course. I figured I would encounter difficult pieces of literature and be expected to write 10-page analysis reports which is slightly intimidating to most students. Contrary to my expectations, enrolling in English 182 has allowed me to understand how people learn in multiple forms and explore different ways of communicating information to others.

For the final portfolio, I wanted to create a theme. Not just to show my understanding, but to also have a little fun during the process. I realized I could connect the outcomes to a few major holidays I was looking forward to, which ironically happen during, or slightly after, the fall quarter (it’s funny because I am taking this class during the fall). Throughout this portfolio, each outcome will be connected to a festivity and one of my projects from this course. I know we’re all excited for the holidays, so why not appreciate it with some enjoyable English concepts. So let’s walk through the autumn season as we reminisce about the holidays everyone loves, and of course, the projects I’ve created during this period.

Using an ePortfolio

  • Introduction
    ePortfolios are a place to demonstrate your work. They are made of sections and pages. The list of sections are along the left side of the window (show me). Each section can have multiple pages, shown on the right side of the window (show me).
  • Portfolio Sections

    Sections are listed along the left side of the window (show me). Each section can have multiple pages inside of it. To organize or edit a section, click the .

    You can rename any section by clicking "Rename", rearrange sections by clicking "Move To...", or delete sections by clicking "Delete" in the popup menu.

  • Section Pages

    Sections have multiple pages. You can see the list of pages for the current section on the right side of the window (show me). To organize or edit a page, click the .

    You can rename any section by clicking "Rename", rearrange sections by clicking "Move To...", or delete sections by clicking "Delete" in the popup menu.

  • Page Content
    The content you see on a page is the same content any visitors will see. To edit this content, click the " Edit This Page" link (show me) and the page will change to editing mode.
  • Page Content

    Now you're editing! Rename the page or change commenting options (show me) if you like. You can save, preview or cancel your changes at any time by clicking the button on the right side (show me).

    Content is divided into subsections, which each have a dotted border. You can delete or edit the contents of a subsection by clicking the or icons at the top right corner of the subsection.

    To add new subsections, find and click the type of content you want to add in the options list on the right side of the page (show me).

  • ePortfolio Settings
    To change the settings for your ePortfolio, click the "ePortfolio Settings" link (show me). You can rename the portfolio and also change whether it is public or private. Private portfolios are only visible to those to whom you grant access.
